## Overview

Organizations continue to face recurring cyber threats that disrupt operations, expose sensitive data, and create significant financial losses. Industry reports consistently identify ransomware, social engineering, and denial-of-service attacks as some of the most common and impactful threats.

This brief outlines these key threat categories and provides practical security investments that organizations can implement to reduce risk.

---

## Key Threats

### 1. Ransomware

Ransomware attacks encrypt or lock critical systems and data, then demand payment for restoration. Industry reporting shows that ransomware continues to be heavily associated with financially motivated cybercriminal activity and remains one of the most damaging forms of cyberattack.

---

### 2. Social Engineering

Social engineering attacks manipulate users into revealing credentials, approving fraudulent requests, or exposing sensitive information. Common techniques include phishing emails, business email compromise (BEC), and deceptive login prompts.

---

### 3. Distributed Denial-of-Service (DDoS)

DDoS attacks overwhelm systems or networks with excessive traffic, making services unavailable to legitimate users. While these attacks may not always result in data breaches, they can cause major operational disruptions and downtime.

---

## Defensive Actions

### Ransomware Protections

- Maintain regular, tested backups that are isolated from production systems  
- Patch operating systems and applications consistently to reduce exposure to known vulnerabilities  

---

### Social Engineering Protections

- Train employees to recognize and report suspicious emails and requests  
- Implement multi-factor authentication (MFA) to protect against credential theft  

---

### DDoS Protections

- Use traffic filtering and rate limiting to distinguish legitimate from malicious traffic  
- Leverage cloud-based mitigation services to handle large-scale attacks  

---

## Business Impact

Failing to invest in cybersecurity can lead to significant financial loss, operational downtime, and reputational damage. Recovery from ransomware attacks alone can cost millions of dollars due to system restoration, lost business, and remediation efforts.

---

## Recommendation

Organizations should treat cybersecurity as a core business investment rather than a reactive expense. Proactive investments in security controls, employee awareness, identity protection, and system resilience are far more cost-effective than responding to a major cyber incident.
