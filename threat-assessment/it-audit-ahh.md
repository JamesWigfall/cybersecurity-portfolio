# IT Audit and Risk Assessment – A Healthy Hand (AHH)

**Prepared by:** James Wigfall  

---

## Overview

A Healthy Hand (AHH) is a non-profit organization that leverages technology such as cloud computing, artificial intelligence, and mobile applications to support its mission of delivering food and shelter to those in need. 

Due to the organization’s reliance on technology, a comprehensive IT audit is essential. This assessment focuses on identifying critical assets, evaluating potential threats, and analyzing vulnerabilities to strengthen overall security.

---

## Asset Identification

The first phase of the IT audit involves identifying key technology assets within the organization. These assets were determined through documentation review and analysis of system functionality.

### Key Assets

1. **Cloud Systems** – Core platform for data storage and service delivery  
2. **AI Analytics Systems** – Supports data-driven decision-making  
3. **Mobile Application** – Enables donor engagement and fundraising  
4. **VR Training Modules** – Provides training for volunteers  
5. **Partnership Management Software** – Facilitates collaboration with external organizations  

---

## Threat Assessment

After identifying assets, the next step is to evaluate potential threats that could impact the organization.

### Threat Types

- **Data Breach**  
  Unauthorized access to sensitive data by external attackers or insiders  

- **Phishing Attacks**  
  Social engineering attempts targeting employees to obtain credentials or sensitive information  

- **Natural Disasters**  
  Environmental events (e.g., floods, fires) impacting physical infrastructure  

---

## Vulnerability Analysis

Understanding both assets and threats allows for identification of key vulnerabilities within the organization.

### Identified Vulnerabilities

- **Weak Cloud Encryption (High Severity)**  
  Risk of data exposure due to insufficient encryption controls  

- **Inadequate Staff Training (Medium Severity)**  
  Employees may fall victim to phishing or mishandle sensitive data  

- **Outdated VR Training Software (Low Severity)**  
  Potential for exploitation due to unpatched software  

---

## Assumptions

- Standard cloud service providers are in use  
- Staff cybersecurity awareness is currently limited  
- Core systems (cloud, AI, mobile) are critical to operations  

---

## Recommendations

To improve the organization’s security posture, the following actions are recommended:

- Implement strong encryption and multi-factor authentication (MFA)  
- Conduct regular cybersecurity awareness training for employees  
- Keep all systems and applications updated with the latest patches  
- Perform routine vulnerability assessments and penetration testing  

---

## Conclusion

This IT audit highlights the importance of a structured approach to identifying assets, assessing threats, and mitigating vulnerabilities. By implementing these recommendations, AHH can better protect its technology infrastructure and ensure the continued delivery of its mission-critical services.
